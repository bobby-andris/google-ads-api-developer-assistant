Allied Brass — Strategic Performance Report: Category & SKU Deep Dive

Date: December 30, 2025
Objective: Optimize 85k SKU catalog by aligning search intent with Business Unit (BU) and Parent Product performance.

1. Executive Summary: Category-Aware Optimization

The Allied Brass account is currently managed via a triple-priority (High/Med/Low) structure segmented by product category. Our deep-dive audit has identified that while the structure is sound, Intent Leakage is occurring in the experimental ("Tst") campaigns, where broad brand terms are being captured at inefficient costs.

Key Finding: Generic terms are often category-specific "Heroes." For example, "valet rod" is a massive profit driver for the Garment Rods BU (32.78 ROAS). Account-wide negative keywords would be catastrophic; we must move to Campaign-Level Sculpting.

3. Targeting Integrity: The "Tst" Campaign Bleed

Our audit revealed that the "Bidnamic - Shopping - US - tst" campaigns are capturing high volumes of brand-specific traffic (allied brass) at generic bids.

  • The Problem: Brand searches in test campaigns dilute the testing data and increase the average CPC for your own name.
  • The Solution: Add [ allied brass ] and [ alliedbrass ] as Exact Match Negatives only to the "Tst" and "EXTRA_LOW" campaigns. This forces traffic to your Branded campaigns.

5. Revised 72-Hour Action Plan (Category-Specific)

A. Brand Protection (Immediate)

  • Negate brand terms in all "Tst" and "EXTRA_LOW" priority campaigns to consolidate brand traffic.

B. Parent SKU Scaling

  • Increase budget for the Carolina Collection (Parent 4545073479812) specifically for "Polished Nickel" finishes, which are outperforming the category average by 2x.

C. Waste Pruning (Surgical)

  • Identify SKUs in "Wall Mounted Towel Bars" with zero conversions but >$50 spend in the last 30 days. Focus pruning here first as it is your largest spend category.
